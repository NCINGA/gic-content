**Women and Child Affairs in Sri Lanka**

**Introduction**

The **Ministry of Women and Child Affairs** is responsible for promoting the rights, protection, and well-being of women and children across Sri Lanka. The Ministry works with government institutions, development partners, and community organizations to create a safe, inclusive, and supportive environment where women and children can thrive.

**Key Services**

The Ministry provides a range of services, including:

Protection and support services for women and children.

Child protection and welfare programmes.

Women's empowerment and livelihood development initiatives.

Awareness programmes on gender equality and child rights.

Support for victims of domestic violence and abuse.

Policy development and implementation related to women and child welfare.

**Child Protection**

Protecting children from abuse, neglect, and exploitation is a national priority. The Ministry collaborates with relevant authorities to:

Respond to reports of child abuse.

Provide counselling and rehabilitation services.

Promote safe learning environments.

Strengthen child protection mechanisms at the community level.

**Important:** Anyone who suspects that a child is at risk should report the matter to the relevant authorities immediately.

**Women's Empowerment**

The Ministry supports women by promoting:

Economic empowerment through skills development programmes.

Leadership opportunities in communities.

Equal access to education and employment.

Awareness of legal rights and available support services.

These initiatives aim to improve the social and economic well-being of women throughout the country.

**Family Support Services**

Families can access various support services, including:

Family counselling.

Parenting awareness programmes.

Community-based support initiatives.

Referral services for specialized assistance.

**How to Access Services**

Citizens can obtain information or request assistance by:

Visiting the nearest Divisional Secretariat.

Contacting the relevant Ministry office.

Accessing services through the Citizen Portal.

Referring to official government announcements.

**Additional Information**

The Ministry regularly conducts awareness campaigns, training programmes, and community outreach activities to improve the quality of life of women and children. Citizens are encouraged to stay informed through official government communication channels and participate in programmes available in their local communities.
