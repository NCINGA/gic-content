# **1. About CeylonCare Medical Holdings**

CeylonCare Medical Holdings (Pvt) Ltd (CMH) is a privately held healthcare group incorporated in Sri Lanka, with a founding mission to deliver accessible, high-quality medical care to patients across urban and suburban communities. Established in 2008 by a consortium of Sri Lankan medical professionals and private investors, the Group has grown from a single diagnostic centre in Colombo into a diversified healthcare network spanning hospitals, specialist outpatient clinics, diagnostic laboratories, and pharmacy retail outlets across the Western, Central, and Southern Provinces.

The Group operates under a unified brand identity and clinical governance framework that prioritises patient safety, clinical excellence, and environmental responsibility. CMH's growth strategy has been shaped by a deliberate commitment to developing healthcare infrastructure in areas underserved by the public health system, and to maintaining the highest standards of facility hygiene and patient environment across all sites — a commitment that elevates the importance of professional facilities cleaning services within the Group's operations to a level that is strategic rather than merely operational.

CMH is a registered private healthcare provider under the National Medicines Regulatory Authority of Sri Lanka and holds accreditation for three of its hospital facilities from the Sri Lanka Standards Institution. The Group employs approximately 2,400 full-time and part-time staff across all sites, including 340 doctors and specialists, 680 nursing and clinical staff, and 1,380 administrative, technical, and facilities personnel. Total Group revenue for the financial year ending March 2025 was approximately LKR 9.2 billion. The Group is wholly privately held; there are no publicly listed entities within the CMH corporate structure.

# **2. Group Facility Portfolio**

## **2.1 Hospital Network**

CMH's core business is anchored by four hospitals operating under the CeylonCare brand, located in Colombo (two facilities), Kandy, and Matara. Together these hospitals provide inpatient, outpatient, surgical, emergency, intensive care, and diagnostic services across a broad range of medical and surgical specialties.

The flagship facility, CeylonCare Central Hospital, is located in Narahenpita, Colombo 05, and is the Group's largest and most complex clinical asset. Opened in 2013, the Central Hospital is an eleven-storey structure with a total gross floor area of approximately 210,000 square feet. It houses 280 inpatient beds, 6 operating theatres (including one hybrid theatre), a Level 3 Intensive Care Unit with 24 beds, a Neonatal Intensive Care Unit, a 24-hour Emergency Department, a comprehensive diagnostic imaging suite, a haematology and biochemistry laboratory, a blood bank, a central sterile services department, and a ground-floor pharmacy. The Central Hospital operates on a 24-hour, 365-day basis, with an average daily patient footfall (inpatient admissions, outpatient appointments, and emergency presentations) of approximately 1,800 persons.

CeylonCare West Hospital, also in Colombo (Malabe, Colombo suburbs), is a seven-storey facility of approximately 145,000 square feet that opened in 2018. It focuses on elective surgical services, maternity, paediatrics, and outpatient specialist consultations, and has 180 inpatient beds. It operates a full theatre suite (4 theatres) and a Day Surgery Unit. Average daily patient footfall at CeylonCare West is approximately 1,100 persons.

CeylonCare Kandy Hospital (established 2016) is a five-storey building of approximately 88,000 square feet with 120 inpatient beds, serving the Central Province catchment. CeylonCare Matara Hospital (established 2020) is a four-storey facility of approximately 62,000 square feet with 90 inpatient beds, serving the Southern Province. Both regional hospitals offer 24-hour emergency services, inpatient wards, surgical services, and outpatient clinics.

## **2.2 Specialist Outpatient Clinics**

CMH operates a network of eight CeylonCare Specialist Centres — branded outpatient-only facilities offering specialist consultations, minor procedures, and diagnostic services without inpatient capabilities. These centres are located in Nugegoda, Bambalapitiya, Battaramulla, Wattala, Kurunegala, Gampaha, Galle, and Negombo. Each Specialist Centre occupies a dedicated floor or building, with a typical gross floor area between 4,500 and 9,000 square feet. Collectively the eight Specialist Centres accommodate approximately 1,600 outpatient appointments per day. The Centres operate Monday to Saturday, 08:00 to 20:00, with select Centres open Sunday mornings.

## **2.3 CeylonCare Diagnostics**

CeylonCare Diagnostics is the Group's network of standalone diagnostic laboratories and medical imaging centres, comprising six collection and processing facilities. Four of the six are co-located within Specialist Centres; two are standalone facilities in Kaduwela and Horana. The Diagnostics network processes approximately 4,200 patient samples per day Group-wide. These facilities include phlebotomy collection areas, sample processing laboratories, histopathology rooms, and medical imaging suites (X-ray, ultrasound, and MRI at the standalone sites). Laboratory environments in Diagnostics facilities require stringent cleaning and infection control protocols due to the nature of biological samples processed.

## **2.4 CeylonCare Pharmacy Retail**

CeylonCare Pharmacy operates 12 retail pharmacy outlets, of which 6 are located within hospital or Specialist Centre buildings and 6 are standalone community pharmacy premises. Individual pharmacy areas range from approximately 600 to 2,400 square feet. Pharmacy premises require clean, professional presentation standards appropriate to a healthcare retail environment, including daily floor care, surface sanitisation, and glass cleaning.

## **2.5 Group Headquarters**

CMH's corporate headquarters is located at CeylonCare Business Park, Nawala Road, Rajagiriya, Colombo 05 — a dedicated five-floor office building of approximately 28,000 square feet housing the Group's corporate functions including finance, human resources, procurement, marketing, legal, and IT. The headquarters building is a standard corporate office environment with no clinical activity.

# **3. Operational Context and Cleaning Service History**

Cleaning and environmental hygiene management across CMH's facility portfolio is governed by the Group's Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) Policy, which is maintained by the Group IPC Committee, chaired by a Consultant Microbiologist. The IPC Policy sets mandatory hygiene standards for all facility types within the Group, with the highest standards applicable to surgical, ICU, neonatal, and laboratory environments. The Group's hospitals are subject to periodic IPC audits conducted by the Ministry of Health's Healthcare Facility Regulation Division.

CMH has historically managed cleaning services through a mixed model: inpatient ward cleaning and operating theatre environments in the Central Hospital and West Hospital have been delivered by dedicated in-house cleaning teams employed directly by CMH, while outpatient areas, specialist centres, diagnostics facilities, pharmacies, and the headquarters building have been served by external cleaning contractors. This structure was adopted in the early years of the Group's growth when the in-house teams provided the flexibility and control required in critical clinical areas. As the portfolio has grown and the operational complexity of managing a mixed model across 21 sites has increased, the Group's executive leadership has approved a transition to a fully outsourced, professionally managed cleaning service model for the forthcoming contract period.

The Group's procurement team has concluded from a market assessment that the complexity and clinical sensitivity of CMH's cleaning requirements — particularly in hospital environments, laboratory facilities, and operating theatres — demands a service provider with a demonstrably structured vendor management capability, applicable healthcare cleaning certifications, and a systematic quality assurance process. The new contract will cover all 21 sites and all cleaning categories, with the service provider taking full responsibility for clinical, general, and specialist cleaning across the Group. CMH will retain an internal IPC oversight function through the Group IPC Committee and the Hospital Matrons at each hospital site.

CMH's annual expenditure on cleaning services across all sites under the new fully outsourced model is estimated at LKR 85,000,000 to LKR 105,000,000 per annum, reflecting both the expanded scope and the enhanced service standards required under Group IPC protocols. This estimate is indicative only.

# **4. CeylonCare Corporate Values Relevant to Service Providers**

Service providers engaging with CeylonCare Medical Holdings are expected to demonstrate alignment with the Group's core values in the conduct of their operations. These values are not aspirational statements — they are embedded in how CMH manages its facilities, its staff, and its supplier relationships.

Patient First: Every decision, including facilities management decisions, is evaluated against its impact on patient safety, comfort, and dignity. This means cleaning operations must be conducted with minimal disruption to patient care activities, with full awareness of patient areas and vulnerability, and with absolute priority given to infection prevention standards.

Accountability: CMH holds its service providers to the same standards of transparency and performance accountability that it applies internally. Service providers are expected to maintain and disclose accurate performance data, to report incidents promptly, and to take ownership of corrective actions without waiting for client direction.

Respect: All personnel present in CMH facilities — whether employed by CMH or by a service provider — are expected to treat patients, visitors, and clinical staff with respect and to observe the confidentiality and privacy standards that healthcare environments demand.

Sustainability: CMH is committed to reducing the environmental footprint of its operations, including the procurement of cleaning services. The Group's Environmental Policy sets targets for the reduction of chemical waste, packaging waste, and water consumption associated with facilities management activities.
