Introduction about the Product 
The GIC is a digital platform developed for GovTech to provide citizens with easy access to 
government information, services, and assistance through a centralized and user-friendly 
interface. The platform aims to improve information accessibility, enhance citizen 
engagement, and streamline the management and delivery of government-related content. 
 
The GIC solution consists of two primary components, 
 
I. 
GIC – Citizen Portal with AI Chat Assistant 
 
The GIC Citizen Portal serves as a public-facing platform through which citizens can access 
government information, services, announcements, and other relevant resources. The portal 
is designed to provide an intuitive and accessible user experience, enabling users to easily 
discover and navigate government information. 
A key feature of the Citizen Portal is the AI Chat Assistance capability, which allows users to 
interact with an intelligent virtual assistant. The AI assistant helps users find relevant 
information, answer queries, guide users to appropriate services, and provide quick access 
to available government resources. 
 
II. 
GIC – Content Management System (CMS) 
 
The GIC - CMS is the administrative platform used by authorized government personnel to 
create, manage, review, approve, and publish content that is displayed on the Citizen Portal. 
The CMS provides tools for content authoring, content governance, and publication control, 
ensuring that information presented to citizens remains accurate, consistent, and up to date. 
 
Together, Citizen Portal and CMS form a comprehensive Government Information Center that 
enables efficient content management while providing citizens with a modern, AI-assisted 
channel for accessing government information and services. The platform supports digital 
transformation initiatives by improving the availability, discoverability, and management of 
public information across government institutions.

2 Purpose of the Manual 
 
This User Manual provides guidance for authorized users responsible for using the GIC - CMS 
and GIC – Citizen Portal with AI Chat Assistant, which were delivered under Phase 1 of the 
project. It serves as a reference document that explains the purpose of the platform, its key 
features, and the procedures required to perform day-to-day activities within the system. 
 
The GIC - CMS and GIC – Citizen Portal with AI Chat Assistant is designed to support the 
collection, management, review, approval, storage, and delivery of verified government 
information through a centralized and structured digital platform. The solution enables 
government institutions to manage information efficiently while providing citizens with 
access to accurate and approved government information through a multilingual 
conversational interface. 
 
This manual describes the functions available within the Phase 1 implementation. The manual 
also explains the responsibilities of different user personas and provides step by step 
guidance for completing common tasks within the system. 
 
The objective of this manual is to help users understand how to use the platform effectively, 
ensure that operational procedures are performed correctly and consistently, and support 
the successful management of government information throughout its lifecycle. It also serves 
as a training and reference guide for system users. 
 
This manual covers only the functionality delivered under Phase 1 of the project.

Content Types 
The Government Information Center (GIC) Content Management System (CMS) enables the 
collection, management, and publication of government information to the Citizen Portal, 
making it accessible to the general public. Information can be added by uploading 
documents, creating questions and answers, or automatically collecting content the 
websites. 
 
The system supports the following content types, 
 
 
Figure 4.4.1 - Content Types 
 
• 
Articles – Content items that contain detailed information in the form of documents. 
Articles are used to publish government information, announcements, guidelines, and 
other informational content on the Citizen Portal. 
 
• 
FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions) – Content items that consist of commonly asked 
questions and their corresponding answers. FAQs help citizens quickly find answers to 
frequently requested information. 
• 
Schedulers – Configurations that automatically collect content from websites at 
scheduled intervals. Each successfully scraped URL is converted into a separate Article in 
the GIC CMS for review, editing, approval, and publication to the Citizen Portal.

All content types in the Government Information Center (GIC) Content Management System 
follow a defined lifecycle from creation to publication. Once content is added to the system, 
it moves through different stages. Each stage is represented by a status that shows the 
current progress of the content. 
 
Content is created by a Content Manager and then follows a multi-level approval process 
before it is published. First, the content is reviewed and approved by the Department Admin. 
It is then reviewed by the GovTech Admin for final approval. Once both approval stages are 
completed, the content is published to the Citizen Portal, where it becomes accessible to the 
general public. 
 
The content lifecycle statuses are as follows, 
• 
Draft – Content is being created and is not yet submitted for review. 
 
• 
Queued – Content has been submitted and is waiting to be processed and moved into 
the approval workflow. 
 
• 
Submitted – Content has entered the approval workflow and is on its way to the first 
reviewer (Department Admin). 
 
• 
Dept Review – The Department Admin is currently reviewing the content as the first 
approval stage. It can be approved or rejected. 
 
• 
Dept Approved – The content has been approved by the Department Admin and moves 
to the next level of review. 
 
• 
GovTech Review – The GovTech Admin is performing the final review before publication. 
It can be approved or rejected. 
• 
Published – The content has been fully approved and is now live on the Citizen Portal. It 
can later be updated (creating a new revision) or retired. 
 
• 
Rejected – The content has been rejected during any review stage (Department Admin 
or GovTech Admin). 
 
• 
Retired – The content has been removed from active publication and is no longer 
available on the Citizen Portal for public access. It is kept for record purposes only.

Age and Embedding 
 
To view the Age and Embedding Status columns, navigate to the Article list view (Navigation 
- Login → Dashboard → Articles). These columns are displayed in the article table, providing 
information about the approval waiting time and AI embedding status for each article. 
 
 
Age 
The Age column helps users identify how long a content item has been waiting for action 
while it is in the approval workflow. For content in the Draft, Department Review, and GovTech 
Review statuses, the system displays the waiting time using colour indicators to help users 
quickly identify items that require attention. Green indicates items waiting for up to 7 days, 
yellow for 8–14 days, orange for 15–29 days, and red for 30 days or more. This helps prevent 
content from being overlooked and ensures timely review and approval. For content in all 
other statuses, the column displays the current status instead of the waiting time. 
 
Embedding Status 
The Embedding Status column indicates whether the content has been processed and 
added to the AI knowledge base, allowing it to be discovered by the AI-powered search and 
assistant. After content is created or updated, it goes through an embedding process before 
it becomes searchable by the AI. The status may appear as Pending (waiting to be 
processed), Processing (currently being added), Done (successfully added and available for 
AI search), or Failed (the embedding process was unsuccessful and requires a retry). 
Monitoring this status helps users ensure that published content is available through AI-
powered search and responses.
