# **1. Scope of Services — Overview**

This document defines the complete scope of cleaning and environmental hygiene services required by CeylonCare Medical Holdings (Pvt) Ltd across all 21 sites in its facility portfolio. The successful service provider will be responsible for delivering and managing all cleaning activities — routine, periodic, reactive, and specialist — across the entire Group portfolio from the contract commencement date of 1 September 2025. The scope described in this document reflects CMH's transition from a mixed in-house and outsourced model to a fully managed outsourced arrangement, as described in REF01.

The service provider must maintain CMH's Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) standards at all times across all sites, with the recognition that the consequences of cleaning failure in a healthcare environment extend beyond inconvenience to direct patient safety risk. The Group IPC Committee, through each hospital's Matron and the Group IPC Coordinator, will retain an oversight role in monitoring cleaning standards and compliance with clinical zone protocols. The service provider's operational management must interface regularly with the Group IPC Committee and respond to IPC-initiated corrective requests as a contractual obligation.

# **2. Facility Zones and Cleaning Classifications**

For the purposes of service specification and pricing, CMH classifies all cleanable spaces across its portfolio into four zone categories, each carrying distinct protocol requirements, product specifications, and minimum service frequencies. These zone categories are aligned with the Australian/New Zealand healthcare cleaning standard AS/NZS 4146:2000 (as adapted for CMH's operational context) and with Sri Lanka Ministry of Health infection control guidance.

## **Zone 1 — Critical Clinical Areas**

Zone 1 covers all spaces with the highest infection control risk, where cleaning failure carries a direct patient safety consequence. Zone 1 spaces within the CMH portfolio include: operating theatres and their preparation and recovery zones; the ICU and HDU at Central Hospital and West Hospital; the Neonatal ICU; the Central Sterile Services Department; isolation rooms and negative-pressure rooms; the blood bank; and all sterile preparation areas. Total Zone 1 space across the portfolio is approximately 48,000 square feet, concentrated primarily at Central Hospital and West Hospital.

Zone 1 cleaning requires the use of hospital-grade sporicidal disinfectants applied to all horizontal surfaces, high-touch points, and equipment contact surfaces, and terminal cleaning protocols after every patient discharge, procedure, or shift end. Colour-coded cleaning equipment is mandatory in Zone 1, with dedicated equipment sets that do not leave the zone. All operatives assigned to Zone 1 must have completed CMH's mandatory Healthcare Cleaning Induction (minimum 8 hours of theory and practical training, delivered by the Group IPC Coordinator) before their first deployment and must undertake annual refresher training. Zone 1 cleaning logs must be completed after every cleaning event and are subject to review by the Hospital Matron.

## **Zone 2 — High-Risk Clinical Areas**

Zone 2 encompasses inpatient wards (general, surgical, maternity, paediatric), the emergency department, outpatient procedure rooms, phlebotomy areas, all patient washrooms and bathrooms within hospital buildings, and the Diagnostics laboratories. Total Zone 2 space across the portfolio is approximately 198,000 square feet. Zone 2 cleaning requires twice-daily surface disinfection of all high-touch points (bed rails, call buttons, door handles, nurse station desks) using a quaternary ammonium compound or equivalent hospital-grade disinfectant. Floor care in inpatient wards uses a two-bucket mopping system with a diluted floor disinfectant. Patient washrooms require a minimum of three cleaning cycles per day in occupied wards. The Diagnostics laboratories require cleaning at the end of each working session using protocols approved by the Laboratory Manager to avoid contamination of biological samples.

## **Zone 3 — General Clinical and Support Areas**

Zone 3 covers all remaining areas within hospital buildings that are not classified as Zone 1 or Zone 2: outpatient waiting areas, reception and registration desks, hospital corridors and lift lobbies, staff rooms and offices, food service areas (including hospital canteens and patient meal preparation staging areas), hospital retail pharmacy areas, and basement and car park areas of hospital buildings. Total Zone 3 space in hospital buildings is approximately 259,000 square feet. Zone 3 cleaning uses standard commercial-grade disinfectant cleaning products. Outpatient waiting areas require particular attention during busy clinic hours, with a minimum of two additional targeted surface wipe-down sweeps performed during the morning and afternoon peaks in addition to routine daily cleaning.

## **Zone 4 — Non-Clinical and Administrative Areas**

Zone 4 includes all Specialist Centres, standalone Diagnostics collection areas, standalone Pharmacy outlets, and the Group Headquarters building. These environments, while still requiring clean and professional presentation appropriate to a healthcare-adjacent setting, do not carry the same clinical risk as hospital zones and are serviced with standard commercial cleaning protocols. Total Zone 4 space across the portfolio is approximately 98,000 square feet. Specialist Centres and standalone facilities operate standard commercial cleaning frequencies, with daily cleaning before facility opening and reactive cleaning available during operating hours.

# **3. Specific Service Requirements by Facility Type**

## **3.1 Hospital Operating Theatres**

Cleaning of operating theatres is the most technically demanding element of this contract and will be assessed with particular rigour during the evaluation of proposals. Theatres require: a between-case clean completed within 15 minutes of the previous case concluding (to allow for theatre turnover without schedule disruption); a daily terminal clean performed at the end of the operating list using a full sporicidal protocol; and a weekly enhanced terminal clean including ceiling, light fittings, walls, and all equipment surfaces. The service provider must demonstrate that its theatre cleaning personnel are trained specifically in theatre environment cleaning and are aware of the sterility requirements and behavioural protocols applicable in this setting. The Group IPC Coordinator must approve all theatre cleaning methodologies and products before they are implemented.

## **3.2 Hospital Canteen and Patient Catering Areas**

Each of the four hospitals operates a patient catering facility and a staff/visitor canteen. Patient meal preparation staging areas (where trolley delivery preparation occurs) are classified as Zone 2. Staff and visitor canteens are Zone 3. Canteen cleaning is conducted in coordination with CMH's contracted catering concessionaire at each hospital. The cleaning service provider is responsible for the cleaning of front-of-house seating, serving areas, and floors; the catering concessionaire is responsible for kitchen equipment and food contact surfaces. This shared-responsibility model requires clear written demarcation agreed between the service provider and the catering team before service commencement. All canteen-related cleaning operatives must hold a valid food handlers' health certificate issued by the relevant Municipal Health Authority.

## **3.3 Specialist Centres and Diagnostics**

Specialist Centres present a moderate cleaning challenge: they are healthcare environments with patient consultation rooms, waiting areas, and minor procedure rooms, but without inpatient wards or operating theatres. Consultation rooms require daily cleaning before the clinic opens and a targeted surface disinfection clean between morning and afternoon sessions. Waiting areas must be visibly clean and free of used materials (used hand sanitiser sachets, paper, tissue) throughout operating hours. The standalone Diagnostics facilities at Kaduwela and Horana include phlebotomy collection areas and sample processing rooms that are classified as Zone 2, requiring appropriate disinfection protocols and coordination with laboratory staff to avoid disruption to sample integrity.

# **4. Waste Management**

CMH generates multiple waste streams across its facility portfolio, including regulated clinical waste (sharps, infected materials, pathological waste), pharmaceutical waste, general non-clinical waste, recyclable dry waste, and food/organic waste. The management of regulated clinical and pharmaceutical waste is the responsibility of CMH's contracted specialist clinical waste contractor (a separate agreement). The cleaning service provider is responsible for the management of general and recyclable waste streams only, including collection from point of generation, transfer to designated waste staging areas, and segregation into the appropriate stream containers.

The service provider must not handle, move, or transport regulated clinical waste (sharps containers, clinical waste bags, pharmaceutical waste containers) under any circumstances. Operatives must be trained to identify clinical waste packaging and to escalate to the facility supervisor if clinical waste is found in an incorrect location. The service provider is also responsible for maintaining the cleanliness of waste staging areas and external bin enclosures at each site, including pressure washing of bin enclosures on a monthly basis.

# **5. Materials, Chemicals, and Equipment**

CMH requires the service provider to supply all cleaning materials, chemicals, equipment, and consumables required to deliver the contracted scope. All chemical products used in Zone 1 and Zone 2 spaces must be on the CMH Group Approved Product List, which is maintained by the Group IPC Coordinator. Any product not currently on the Approved Product List must be submitted for review and approval before use; this process takes a minimum of 10 working days. The service provider must therefore submit its proposed product list for all zones to the Group IPC Coordinator at least 4 weeks before contract commencement.

Products used in Zone 3 and Zone 4 spaces must comply with the Central Environmental Authority's standards for chemical usage and disposal. The Group has a target of sourcing at least 65% of all cleaning chemicals (by volume) from environmentally certified suppliers by the end of the first contract year. The service provider is required to report quarterly on the proportion of products meeting this certification standard.

Minimum equipment requirements by zone include: Zone 1 — dedicated microfibre equipment sets per theatre (colour-coded, single use or high-temperature laundered daily), steam cleaning units for floor care; Zone 2 — two-bucket mop systems per ward, HEPA-filtered vacuum cleaners for carpeted staff areas; Zone 3 and Zone 4 — commercial scrubber-dryers for large common areas, standard vacuum cleaners and mop systems, washroom hygiene equipment.

# **6. Staffing, Supervision, and Conduct Standards**

All personnel deployed on CMH premises must comply with CMH's Facilities Personnel Code of Conduct, a copy of which will be provided at contract commencement. Key requirements include: wearing a clearly visible, professional uniform with the service provider's name and the individual's name displayed; maintaining patient confidentiality and never discussing patient information observed in the course of work; respecting patient privacy at all times in clinical areas; refraining from using personal mobile phones or recording devices in patient areas; and adhering to CMH's visitor management and infection control procedures (including hand hygiene compliance and wearing of face masks in Zone 1 and Zone 2 spaces as required by the IPC Committee).

A Police Clearance Certificate (issued within six months of deployment) is required for all personnel deployed in inpatient ward areas, ICU, NICU, and operating theatre environments. The service provider must maintain records of all Police Clearance Certificates for deployed personnel and make them available to CMH's HR Division upon request.

The service provider is required to designate a named Site Supervisor at each hospital site (4 supervisors), with the Site Supervisor present during the morning cleaning shift and contactable during all operating hours. A Group Contract Manager — the service provider's senior representative for the CMH account — must be reachable during business hours and must attend monthly contract review meetings with CMH's Group Facilities Manager.

# **7. Performance Standards and KPIs**

The service provider will be held to the following minimum Key Performance Indicators throughout the contract term. Performance will be assessed monthly through CMH's internal quality inspection programme (conducted by the Group Facilities Manager and Matrons at hospital sites) and biannually through a formal client satisfaction survey distributed to Hospital Matrons and Specialist Centre administrators.

# **8. Reporting and Contract Management**

The service provider is required to provide monthly performance reports to CMH's Group Facilities Manager by the 8th of the following month. Reports must cover: task completion rates by site and zone; internal quality inspection scores; incident and near-miss log summary; consumables usage; any corrective actions initiated or closed; and Zone 1 and Zone 2 cleaning log compliance rates. Quarterly reports must additionally include waste volume data by stream, chemical product certification percentage, and a workforce compliance summary confirming EPF/ETF and employment law compliance for all deployed personnel.

Contract review meetings will be held monthly between the Group Contract Manager (service provider) and CMH's Group Facilities Manager. Quarterly reviews will additionally include the Group IPC Coordinator and a representative from the Hospital Matrons' group. The service provider must be prepared to present performance data, trend analysis, and any proposed service improvements at each quarterly review.
